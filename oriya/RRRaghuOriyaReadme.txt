Introduction - RaghuOriya

12 Indian languages. These fonts named as RaghuHindi, RaghuTamil etc.  have been
designed,  developed and realised through the efforts of the following people of

RaghuOriya  is a digital font, in OpenType Format using Unicode standards and is
based on visual syllable standard of IndiX Compugraphy, for  creating,  editing,
viewing and printing text in Oriya script for Oriya language.

RaghuOriya  font Original Version 1.00 (2005) has been designed and developed by
Prof. R. K. Joshi (TypeFont Design Director, Visiting Design  Specialist  at  C-
DAC  Mumbai)  in  association  with  Mr. Rajith Kumar K. M. (TypeFont Designer),
assisted by Mr. Nirmal Biswas, Ms. Jui Mhatre and Ms. Supriya Kharkar at C-  DAC
Mumbai under IndiX2, Project funded by TDIL Programme, Department of Information
Technology, Ministry of Communications  and  Information  Technology,  Govt.  of
India.

In  RaghuOriya  font  the  sufficient  number  of glyphs have been provided. The
designed letterforms of RaghuOriya font in Oriya script, have elegant structures
of  thick  and semi-thick strokes with graceful curves. Vertical strokes contain
slight curvature. This font generally  takes care of written syllables for Oriya
language text using Oriya script.

The actual font is available, among other places, from:

Usage

Using  RaghuOriya  font  to  its  full  potential, requires a text layout system
capable of recognizing Unicode standard and  reordering  as  well  as  combining
characters according to the visual syllable standard of IndiX Compugraphy. Refer
to the standard  available  on  website  http://www.cdacmumbai.in/projects/indix
RaghuOriya  is  to  be  used  for  continuous  text  in  Oriya  language. And is
recommended to be used in minimum 18pt. size on screen and 12pt. size on  paper.
